How Salary Sacrifice Works

When you contribute to your pension through salary sacrifice, your pension contribution is deducted from your gross salary before income tax and National Insurance are calculated. This means you pay less tax and NI, making your actual out-of-pocket cost lower than the percentage you contribute.

For example, on a £20,000 salary, a 5% contribution only costs -56p per £1 because of the combined tax and NI relief. Your employer may also match or exceed your contribution.

The figures above assume a 3% employer pension match, which is common in many schemes. Check your specific workplace pension for exact employer contribution details.
